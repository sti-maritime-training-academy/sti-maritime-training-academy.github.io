# Scope

The course provides participants with a comprehensive understanding of the critical aspects of bulk cargo handling, from calculation techniques to environmental regulations. It covers both theoretical and practical knowledge, with a focus on safety, efficiency, and compliance with international maritime standards.

A total of eight (24) instructional hours, including assessment, are required to complete the course.

# Training Outcomes

This course is designed and suitable for all relevant personnel. Specifically, at the end of the course the trainee must be able to:

- Perform draft survey calculations manually and using Excel tools.
- Conduct NCB (National Cargo Bureau) grain calculations both manually and via Excel.
- Understand and apply the IMSBC Code to bulk cargo operations.
- Comply with MARPOL Annex V regulations, including proper disposal of cargo residues, wash water, and garbage record entries.
- Interpret cargo securing manuals and stability criteria for safe operations.
- Apply voyage instructions effectively to optimize cargo handling.
- Plan and manage loading and discharging sequences to ensure smooth operations.
- Understand load line zones and apply Freeboard, Deadweight, and Allowance (FWA, DWA) rules.
- Implement ballast water management systems and maintain accurate Ballast Water Record Entries.
- Use loading computers (Cargo Max, Load Ace) for stowage planning and cargo management.
- Prepare stowage plans and strategies to optimize space, weight distribution, and safety.

# Entry Standards

This course is specifically designed for seafarers with a Chief Officer license, including both newly certified Chief Officers and incoming Chief Officers. The course is open to individuals currently working or aspiring to work as Chief Officers on ships.

# Course Certificate

Upon successful completion of the course, participants will receive a **Certificate of Training Completion**. This certifies their attendance and completion on the Bulk Carrier Course for Chief officer training.
